# ProductKind Educational Tone & Style

Apply this style when creating educational content, courses, tutorials, or explanatory materials.

## Core Voice Principles

**Warm and encouraging** ‚Äî Write as a supportive mentor, not a lecturer. Use phrases like "Now it's your turn", "Don't worry about getting it perfect", "You've got this!" Celebrate progress genuinely, not performatively.

**Authentic over polished** ‚Äî Avoid marketing buzzwords and corporate speak. Be direct and human. Say "this is hard" when something is hard. Acknowledge struggle openly.

**Curious and exploratory** ‚Äî Frame learning as discovery, not instruction. Invite questions and reflection.

**Transformation-focused** ‚Äî Frame courses and content around identity change, not just skill acquisition. "From 'I can't do X' to 'I just did X'" is more powerful than "Learn how to do X."

---

## The Mentor Voice

Write as if you're sitting next to the learner, guiding them through something new. A good mentor:

**Acknowledges where they are.** "You might be feeling overwhelmed right now. That's completely normal." Don't pretend everything is easy when it isn't.

**Names difficulty explicitly.** Use step titles like "Things will break (and that's okay)" or "This part is tricky." Learners trust content that acknowledges reality.

**Normalises mistakes.** "If something goes wrong, don't worry. This is a normal part of building." Make it safe to fail.

**Celebrates progress genuinely.** "You've come a long way. From your first prompt to a published app." Be specific about what they've achieved, not generic ("Great job!").

**Points forward.** End with momentum: "What do you want to build next?" Send learners into their future, not just through your content.

**Doesn't oversimplify.** Avoid "It's easy!" or "Simply do X." If it were easy, they wouldn't need a course. Respect the learning process.

## Structural Patterns

### Why Before How

Always explain the rationale before the technique:

```
Why start with [concept]? [Explanation of value/importance]. [Connect to user's real problem].

How to [do the thing]: [Practical steps follow]
```

### Challenge-Based Structure

Organise content into numbered challenges or steps that build progressively:

```
Challenge 1: [Action-oriented title]
Challenge 2: [Next building block]
...
```

### Concrete Running Examples

Use a single, relatable persona throughout (like "Sarah, the book club organiser"). Reference them consistently to make abstract concepts tangible.

### Template/Fill-in-the-Blank Approach

Provide frameworks users can complete:

```
I am a [user persona] trying to [achieve outcome], but [barrier] because [root cause], which makes me feel [emotion].
```

### Input Prompts

End sections with clear action items:

```
INPUT: [Specific instruction for what the learner should write/do]
(Example: "I will invite my friend who matches my persona to test the app...")
```

## Language Guidelines

**Use British English throughout** ‚Äì Follow British English grammar and punctiation (e.g. organise, colour).

**Use direct address** ‚Äì "You" and "your" extensively. Write to the person, not about them.

**Explain jargon when introduced** ‚Äì "MVP (Minimum Viable Product)" on first use, then "MVP" thereafter.

**Prefer active voice** ‚Äì "You'll learn" not "This will teach you"

**Keep sentences accessible** ‚Äì Short to medium length. One idea per sentence for complex concepts.

**Use concrete over abstract** ‚Äì "scattered across WhatsApp and email" not "fragmented communication channels"

**Refrain from using em dashes (‚Äî)** ‚Äì Avoid using em dashes for more natural language.

**Avoid AI buzzwords** ‚Äì "why it matters", "here's the thing", "this one's for you", "Not X, but Y", "It's not... it's...", "X isn't... it's..." are off-limits.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ‚ùå Corporate buzzwords ("leverage", "synergy", "optimise")
- ‚ùå Passive, distant tone ("It is recommended that...")
- ‚ùå Walls of text without structure
- ‚ùå Abstract concepts without examples
- ‚ùå Assuming prior knowledge without explanation
- ‚ùå Ending sections without clear next actions
- ‚ùå Excessive emoji use (more than 1-2 per section)
- ‚ùå Condescending simplification ("It's easy!" or "Simply do X" when it's not)
- ‚ùå "Obviously" or "of course" (implies they should already know)
- ‚ùå "Just" before instructions ("Just click the button") ‚Äî minimises the learning
- ‚ùå Hiding difficulty ‚Äî pretending everything is straightforward when it isn't
- ‚ùå Generic encouragement ("Amazing!" "Great job!") without specifics
- ‚ùå Lecturing tone ‚Äî talking at the learner rather than guiding them
