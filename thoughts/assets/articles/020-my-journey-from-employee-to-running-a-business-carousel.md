## LinkedIn caption (exact)

At the end of 2024, I left my full-time job after 10 years in tech, most of it in product management.

I loved the work itself. I was tired of the corporate politics, and of building products executives wanted while the people using them had different ideas.

Since then I've been experimenting with revenue streams: work that fulfils me, has a positive social impact, and pays the bills. That combination turned out to be harder to find than I expected 😅

The slides tell the story: coaching, vibe coding, and a hackathon for women, where I noticed most vibe coding content is made by tech bros, for tech bros. We've seen this before: in 1984, women earned 37% of US computer science degrees, then personal computers were marketed to boys and men, and the numbers dropped. Today, 14% of Lovable's users are women.

So my partner and I have refocused productkind, our two-person company, on closing the gender gap in AI adoption.

The full story, including what definitely didn't work, is in my newsletter, Thoughts by productkind (link in the comments). And if you know someone rethinking their own path, I'd be so grateful if you shared this with them.

## Instagram caption (exact)

At the end of 2024, I left my full-time job after 10 years in tech, most of it in product management. I loved the work. I was tired of the corporate politics, and of building products executives wanted while the people using them had different ideas.

Since then I've tried executive coaching, discovered vibe coding, built products without knowing TypeScript, and joined SheBuilds on Lovable, a 48-hour hackathon for women. There I noticed that most vibe coding content is made by tech bros, for tech bros.

In 1984, women earned 37% of US computer science degrees. Then personal computers were marketed to boys and men, and the share dropped. Today, 14% of Lovable's users are women.

So we've refocused our two-person company, productkind, on closing the gender gap in AI adoption. The full story, including what didn't work, is in my newsletter, link in bio. 💜
