# Research: Substack About page best practices

Researched: 5 July 2026, via targeted web search and source reads. Lighter-weight than the welcome-email research (no adversarial verification pass); treat as guidance, not verified fact.

## Purpose of the About page

Per Substack's official guidance: the About page tells new readers who you are and what you write about at greater length than the one-line publication description. Its goal is to establish credibility while showing off your writing voice and style. Potential subscribers check it before giving an email address, and especially before paying.

## Elements Substack officially recommends

- What the publication is about: topic areas and post types, implying what it does for readers and who it is for.
- A brief personal bio: who you are and why you're qualified.
- Publication logistics: how often you publish and at what price.
- Subscriber benefits: what free and paid subscribers each get.
- Optional polish: images of yourself or your subject matter, testimonials from subscribers, highlighted or unlocked paid posts, and a personal welcome video.
- Edited under Settings > Website > About page.

## Practitioner conversion structure (PubStack Success)

Recommended order:

1. Profile photo: face-forward, smiling, good lighting, to build trust.
2. What subscribers get: frequency, topics, format. Optionally clarify what the publication is NOT about, so the wrong readers disqualify themselves.
3. Your identity: two to four sentences, only what is relevant to the publication.
4. Your mission: why the publication exists.
5. Subscriber benefits: describe how readers' lives improve, not just what they receive.
6. Paid/pledges explanation, if applicable.
7. Two subscribe calls-to-action: one above the fold, one near the bottom.

Optional: links to best posts, ideal-reader descriptions, testimonials, social handles (share handles rather than external links).

Other advice: use subheadings for scannability; match tone and length to the publication's style; avoid external links, which redirect visitors away from the subscribe conversion.
