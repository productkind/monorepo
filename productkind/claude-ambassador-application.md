# Claude Community Ambassador Application

Applicant: Kinga Magyar
Working draft. We add each question and answer as we go.

---

## 1. Briefly tell us why you are interested in becoming a Claude Ambassador

I'm a product manager and co-founder of Little Parrot, a microlearning platform that teaches women who can't code yet to build with AI. Our mission is to close the gender gap in AI adoption, and Claude Code is the tool our two-person company runs on: we use it every day to turn our teaching into courses, emails and content far faster than two people otherwise could. I came to AI from a business background rather than an engineering one, so I know first-hand what it feels like to start, and what helps people I now teach.

Becoming an ambassador would let me do more of what I already do. Over the past year I've supported women through three SheBuilds on Lovable buildathons, and over 600 of them have started one of our free courses. Software is a form of power, and I want the women I teach to hold some of it. Claude is the tool I'd hand them first.

---

## 2. Have you run events or built community before? Tell us about it

Yes, organising communities is a steady part of my life. I'm an organiser of ProductTank Lisbon, the local chapter of one of the largest product management communities in the world. I've organised regular meetups: finding speakers, hosts and sponsors, and running the events themselves.

More recently my community work has centred on women building with AI. I've been part of three SheBuilds on Lovable buildathons: I joined the first cohort as a participant, mentored women in the second through the community Discord, and for the International Women's Day global event in March 2026 we opened our "Build Your First App with Lovable" course to all participants for free.

And Little Parrot itself is a community effort: a kind, welcoming learning space where mistakes are part of learning. Alongside it I write a Substack newsletter for product builders.

Through LinkedIn.

## 4. Anything else we should know?

Two things: 

1. At the SheBuilds event in Singapore this March, we discovered that a Lovable Ambassador had used our course content word-for-word in her own presentation, unprompted, because it taught the tool better than the official materials did. I mention it because that is the job of an ambassador in miniature: making material good enough that other teachers reach for it.

2. And when I say our company runs on Claude Code, I mean well beyond writing code. Our courses are drafted, critiqued by review agents we built, and shipped through it, along with our emails and content. We've written skills that give Claude enough context on what good looks like for us, from our tone of voice to our course pedagogy, so the expertise stays ours and the output stays consistent. This application was written with it too. I'd be representing a tool I use every day, and I'd want to bring what we've built at Little Parrot to the Claude community: a space where women feel welcomed, ask questions freely, and stay. That is how a community becomes diverse.
