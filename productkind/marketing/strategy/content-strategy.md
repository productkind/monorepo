## Positioning and point of view

**Who it's for:** non-technical women with an idea, juggling a job and a life,
who want to build it themselves.

**The promise:** practical, step-by-step courses that fit a coffee break and
end in something real you built.

**The point of view (what only we say):**

- The AI gap is a trust and confidence gap, not an ability gap, and the
  evidence backs it. We design and write for confidence on purpose.
- Learning AI can feel calm. We refuse manufactured urgency; possibility is a
  better motivator than fear of falling behind.
- We show the how. Results shown without method teach nothing; every piece of
  content carries a step someone can take on Monday.
- You don't need to become a developer to build software. Your expertise (the
  problem you understand) is the valuable part; AI is the power tool.
- We are our own proof: a two-person company running a real product business
  with AI doing the production work, showing its workings in public.

One sentence: **Little Parrot teaches women to build their ideas with AI, in a
calm, practical way, from the people who build their own company this way.**

## Content pillars

Every post traces to one pillar. Each pillar names the audience evidence it
serves.

1. **The method, shown.** Real prompts, real steps, real numbers from our own
   work: how we build courses, run marketing, use Claude Code, decide what AI
   may and may not touch. *(Serves: anger at results-without-method; the
   open creator lane; the June calendar proved the format.)*
2. **Women build real things.** Learner and community stories in their native
   arc: permission, surprise, beneficiary. The eNPS tool, the dementia care
   app, the SheBuilds builds. Always with the builder's consent, always
   crediting her. *(Serves: "if she can do it, so can I"; techbro-example
   alienation; the share-driven acquisition loop.)*
3. **The messy middle.** Doom loops, credit anxiety, security basics, "week 4"
   survival: the honest, unglamorous skills nobody else teaches. Diagnosis
   before fixing. *(Serves: the No. 1 user pain; the biggest competitive gap;
   direct ROI framing.)*
4. **The calm view.** Kinga's product-judgement essays: output is not value,
   judgement is the job, learning without urgency, what the gender-gap
   evidence says. Conference-notes posts live here. *(Serves: positioning;
   trust with the peer/partner audience; Substack depth.)*

Rough mix at 3 posts/week: 1 method, 1 messy-middle or story, 1 calm-view,
rotating so stories appear at least twice a month.

## Platform priorities

1. **LinkedIn, Kinga's profile** - primary. Founder voice, first person. The
   audience's main learn-for-career channel and our proven format. Note from
   the research: under ~5K followers, single-image posts outperform document
   carousels; text with one strong image beats video.
2. **Substack (productkind publication + Notes)** - trust deepener. Long-form
   for pillar 4 and method deep-dives; Notes for the reflective middle layer.
   The She Writes AI directory (400+ women writing about AI) and M(AI)VENS /
   Women + AI are the cross-promo and guest-post map.
3. **Communities (SheBuilds Discord, Elpha, Slack/WhatsApp groups, PWIT)** -
   highest-trust channel, per `channels.md` rules. Helpful answers first;
   the messy-middle pillar is community gold (doom-loop rescues, credit
   checklists).
4. **Instagram (productkind)** - discovery. Carousels and checklists reused
   from LinkedIn winners; "how I use AI to [life thing]" wedge content fits
   here best.
5. **Own surfaces (Discord, email)** - retention and share-asks, unchanged.

## Cadence and formats

- **LinkedIn 3x/week** (Mon/Wed/Fri), the proven June rhythm. 90-200 words,
  one real moment, one transferable tip, no hashtags.
- **Substack: one post a month** plus Notes when a thought is ready. The
  monthly newsletter continues as is.
- **Instagram: 1-2x/week**, reusing made assets; never made from scratch.
- **Saveable formats win:** prompt recipes, checklists, before/after builds.
  Toolkit items from courses are pre-made saveable content; reuse them.
- Sustainable for two people means: nothing on this list requires new material
  more than 4 times a week, and a missed slot is a missed slot, not a crisis.

## From viewer to subscriber

The path the content feeds, matching how acquisition works today:

1. **Sees** a post (a learner's story, a method post, a rescue thread) or a
   share from a paid user - the loop that already drives acquisition.
2. **Saves/follows** because the content is concretely reusable.
3. **Starts a free course** (the free-course links are our lead magnet; the
   life-admin course is the lifestyle wedge, Lovable course the builder wedge).
4. **Finishes and shares** - the share-ask is acquisition-critical, and the
   completion moment ("I built this") is the native shareable.
5. **Subscribes** to keep going, framed as momentum ("Keep going"), priced
   against the credits she stops wasting, cancel-anytime stated plainly.

Content's job is steps 1-3. The product handles 4-5.

## What we never do

From the belief interview and the audience evidence: no urgency or fear-based
hooks; no "become a developer" framing; no overselling course outcomes; no
bashing the tools people use; no AI-written voice; no hiding the subscription
behind a one-course promise; no pricing surprises.

## Review

Quarterly, or when something big shifts (a SheBuilds season's results, a
competitor move like SheCodes' pivot, a pillar consistently underperforming).
Ask: which pillar drove saves, shares, and free-course starts? Kill or reshape
the weakest, keep the mix honest.
