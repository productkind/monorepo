# TikTok experiment brief

Status: planned. Written 2026-07-23. Review at the gate (see below).

A time-boxed test of TikTok as a founder-fronted channel for Little Parrot, run
by Kinga. The goal is to build an audience from zero and, once the funnel
unlocks, guide it to Little Parrot.

## Why now, and the hypothesis

Our social efforts so far have not gained much traction, and both our research
and what women tell us point the same way: content where a real person is
present works better. One woman put it plainly: she resonates with women-led,
community-driven brands.

Two findings in our own research back a TikTok test specifically:

1. There is no established female vibe-coding educator anywhere, and short video
   is where that lane is most visibly empty. The content there is either
   polished demo-bro or hollow "corporate girlie" AI content, so substance from
   a real founder stands out.
2. Person-present content beats logo content everywhere (faces, stories,
   opinions), and people follow people. See `../platform-playbook-2026.md`.

**Hypothesis:** a Kinga-fronted, build-in-public TikTok channel can grow a
relevant following faster than our current channels, and cross the 1,000
-follower mark that unlocks a clickable bio link to Little Parrot.

## Decisions locked

- **Account:** Kinga-fronted creator account, Little Parrot as the destination.
  Kinga's name and face lead; Little Parrot is where it points.
- **Account type:** creator/personal, not Business. This keeps trending sounds
  available (a real reach lever early), at the cost of no clickable bio link
  until 1,000 followers (confirmed current as of April 2026; personal accounts
  need 1,000 followers to unlock the website field, Business accounts get it
  immediately but lose trending sounds). For an audience-building test, reach
  matters more than the link in the first weeks, and we will not clear 1,000
  followers for a while regardless.
- **Angle:** how Kinga uses AI to run a two-person team and business, plus
  building digital products. Founder building-in-public, not the "How I use AI
  for [life thing]" lifestyle wedge.
- **Format:** Kinga on camera to open and close, screen recording in the middle,
  real hiccups left in. The imperfection is the differentiator against polished
  demo content.
- **Cadence:** 15 posts over roughly 5 weeks, 3x per week.

## The angle tradeoff, eyes open

Choosing the business/build-in-public angle over the lifestyle wedge changes who
it reaches.

- **Given up:** the lifestyle wedge was the format our research validated as the
  widest top-of-funnel for this audience, including women who would never search
  anything about building software. The business angle reaches fewer casual
  scrollers and risks skewing toward a founder/indie-hacker crowd rather than
  the ICP.
- **Gained:** it is sharper for the "women with a business idea" half of the
  ICP, it is the empty lane our research named (the "teaching the system is the
  system" model), and it is what Kinga can make authentically three times a
  week. A founder-fronted channel lives or dies on the founder wanting to make
  the content.

**Mitigation:** build the bridge into every video. Each post connects "here is
the system I used" to "this is the same skill you would use to build your own
thing." If reach at the gate disappoints, testing a few lifestyle-wedge posts is
the obvious next experiment.

## Success gate and metrics

Review after the first 8-10 posts, decide scale-or-kill by the end of the 15.

- **Primary (decision-makers):** completion rate and sends. These are the
  signals TikTok actually ranks on, and a video someone sends a friend is the
  same motion as sharing a free-course link.
- **Secondary (load-bearing here):** follower growth. Not the success bar, but
  1,000 followers is the threshold that unlocks the bio-link funnel, so it earns
  its place. Watch the trajectory towards 1,000, not a vanity total.
- **Not judged yet:** paid conversions and attributed course starts. The funnel
  is not wired up during this run; judging the channel on them now would be
  unfair.

**Kill/scale decision:** at 15 posts, scale if completion rate and sends are
healthy and follower growth is trending towards 1,000 within a sensible horizon.
Kill or re-angle (lifestyle wedge) if completion and sends are flat regardless
of follower count.

## The funnel, and the parked link decision

There is no clickable bio link until 1,000 followers, so for this run:

- Drive people with a spoken and on-screen CTA plus a plain-text bio mention
  (for example "Free courses at Little Parrot, search Little Parrot AI") and a
  named destination in a pinned comment.
- **Parked, trigger at 1,000 followers:** decide the bio-link destination. Open
  question is whether a free course is evergreen (startable without a SheBuilds
  code) or only made free for events. Options weighed: free course (matches the
  proven share-driven loop), email/newsletter capture (owns the audience
  off-platform), or a lightweight link page combining both. Recommendation when
  the time comes: free course plus an email capture, if a free course is
  evergreen; otherwise the newsletter, and treat "keep an evergreen free course
  for cold social traffic" as its own decision.

## Operating model and cost control

- **Record once, publish twice:** each video goes to TikTok and to Instagram
  Reels (as a Collab between Kinga and the productkind/Little Parrot account,
  never a duplicate re-upload). Screen recordings over produced video.
- **Protect LinkedIn:** LinkedIn stays our primary organic channel at its
  current cadence, untouched, so we can tell whether TikTok added reach or just
  moved Kinga's time around. For a two-person team the founder should not be
  everywhere; TikTok is layered onto the Reels we make anyway, not a third front.
- **Export hygiene:** clean 1080x1920 exports, no TikTok/CapCut watermarks on
  the Reels cross-post.
- **Search metadata:** say the search phrase out loud in the video and put it in
  the caption; 3-5 hashtags max (1 broad + 2-3 niche).

## Risks

- Time drain pulling Kinga off LinkedIn. Mitigated by the record-once model and
  the untouched LinkedIn cadence.
- Angle reaches builders, not the ICP. Mitigated by the bridge in every video
  and the lifestyle-wedge fallback.
- No funnel until 1,000 followers. Accepted; this run is about audience and
  signal, not conversion.
