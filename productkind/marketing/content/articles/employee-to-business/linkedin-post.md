# LinkedIn post: My journey from employee to running a business

Personal post on Kinga's LinkedIn announcing the article and the return to publishing on productkind.substack.com.

- Channel: LinkedIn (personal account)
- Article: https://productkind.substack.com/p/my-journey-from-employee-to-running-a-business
- Status: final, approved 2026-07-09

---

Last year I was writing articles about building products every week, for months. Then life (and a business) got in the way and I stopped. I told myself it was a short break. It turned into over half a year.

Not that I sat still, though. Most of that time went into Little Parrot, our micro-learning platform that teaches women how to build with AI, and into a lot of thinking I never wrote about.

Part of that thinking was about who gets spoken to in all the AI-building content out there. Last year Elena Verna estimated that around 20% of Lovable's users were women, which already felt low. Lovable recently published their own statistics, and the real share is a meagre 14%. It reminds me of the 1980s, when personal computers were marketed to boys and the number of women studying computer science started to fall. The gap comes from who the content speaks to, rather than from ability.

That gap is a big part of why I want to write again. I wrote an article back in January about my journey from employee to running a business, then sat on it. The plan was to have at least five articles ready before I started publishing, so I could keep a regular cadence. I wrote about three, then Little Parrot took priority.

What I've noticed since is that drafts don't age well. I move on, my thinking changes, and my context is different now than it was in January. So I'm publishing, without committing to a weekly cadence. When I post something, I want it to be useful for someone. I'm not a content machine.

This is the first article this year: https://productkind.substack.com/p/my-journey-from-employee-to-running-a-business?utm_source=linkedin-k

I'd love to know what you're struggling with as you build or use AI. That's what I want to write about next.
