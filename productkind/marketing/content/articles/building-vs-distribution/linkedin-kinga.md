# LinkedIn post sharing article 022: Building is cheap(er), but distribution is still expensive

> Swap `[article link]` for the live Substack URL before posting. Keep the link in the post body, not the first comment (LinkedIn penalises author-comment links).

The other week I chatted with Simon Schaefer, one of the initiators of EU-INC, the grassroots campaign for a standard startup incorporation form in the EU. Their petition was signed by almost every famous startup founder in Europe, including the founders of Stripe, Wise, Lovable and ElevenLabs. I had to ask: how did a 10-person team rally that much support? What was their main distribution channel?

LinkedIn.

One side of me was glad to hear this, because we all have access to LinkedIn. The other side was a bit annoyed. Posting here feels like a chore. I hate telling people, "look at me, look what great things I'm making!" 😅

But it confirmed something I've been thinking about a lot while building Little Parrot: building a product is about 25% of the job. The expensive part is distribution, meaning how your target audience finds what you've built.
