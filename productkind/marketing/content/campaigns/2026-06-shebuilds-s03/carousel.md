# Carousel - LinkedIn (parrot gives the advice)

A 5-card carousel for LinkedIn. The parrot is a cheeky guide walking the reader to
the prize. Caption underneath: the finalised `linkedin-personal.md` (slides carry the
visual hook, the caption carries the detail and links).

Substack note: Notes can't do carousels (single image only). Use **Card 1** as a
standalone hero image there, or keep the Substack Note text-only.

---

## Card 1 - hook
- **Parrot:** curious, head tilted
- **Title:** How do you get Elena Verna to mentor you?
- **Subtitle:** Advice from a parrot 🦜

## Card 2 - the prize, and what it is
- **Parrot:** leaning in, among the flock
- **Text:** She's the top prize at SheBuilds, a 48-hour buildathon by Lovable, made for women.

## Card 3 - the course (normally paid, free for this)
- **Parrot:** with a little book
- **Text:** New to Lovable? Build Your First App With Lovable is one of our courses on LittleParrot.app. We've made it free until 23 June.

## Card 4 - apply and share
- **Parrot:** passing something to another parrot
- **Text:** Apply for SheBuilds. And pass the free course to a woman who'd love to build.

## Card 5 - close
- **Parrot:** looking out
- **Text:** Who'd build something brilliant in 48 hours, if no one made her doubt she belonged?
- **Small:** Links in the post 🦜

## Design notes
- **Fonts:** Space Mono for card titles/short lines, Inter for any longer body.
- **Colours:** brand orange `hsl(33 100% 68%)` and green `hsl(148 100% 47%)`; highlight gradient for the title card if wanted.
- **Style:** neobrutalist, sharp edges (0 radius), 2px solid borders, hard box shadow `0.25rem 0.25rem 0.125rem rgba(8 8 9 / 0.25)`. Lay the text in a bordered box over each photo so it stays legible.
- **Format:** 4:5 (1080x1350) to maximise feed space. Upload as a LinkedIn document/carousel post.

## Open decision: the parrot images
Keeping the same parrot across 5 poses is the hard part. Stock photos won't give a
consistent character. Options: an AI-generated parrot (fixed character via prompt/seed)
or an illustrated mascot. Pick this before laying out slides.
