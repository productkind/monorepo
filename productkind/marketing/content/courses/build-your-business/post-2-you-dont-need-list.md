# Post 2: What you don't need to start earning

**Pillar:** the calm view (permission content).
**Research trace:** the audience's native arc starts with permission ("I did
not wait for permission or a co-founder or a computer science degree");
premature setup costs feed the documented cost anxiety, so the $20-30
budget rule is trust-building; deferring company registration is honest,
unglamorous advice nobody else leads with. Draws on the course's opening
"you don't need" list and the Accountant Call Preparation toolkit item.

**Alt text (for the post):** A permission-list carousel about starting to
earn from a self-built product: no registered company, investors,
trademark, or perfect product needed yet; what's needed is users getting
value, a $20-30 budget for domain and email, and an accountant conversation
once revenue arrives.

## Caption (exact)

There's a version of "starting a business" that lives in our heads: register a company, print cards, perfect the product, then finally show it to the world. It's expensive, it's slow, and the order is backwards. 🌱

What Build Your Business: From Vibe Coded Product to Paying Customers teaches first is the shorter list. You need users getting value from what you built. Until that's true, the company registration, the trademark, and the polish are costs without a purpose, and until you have traction, your total spend can stay under $30: a domain and a professional email.

The company question has a calm answer too: register when revenue is solid or a deal requires it, and let an accountant tell you what's right for your country. The course includes the exact questions to ask in that first call.

The course is on LittleParrot.app, and the first challenge is free.
