# Post 6: Picking a price on purpose

**Pillar:** the method, shown.
**Research trace:** pricing is the "earn" step of the dream state (first
paying customer, "be my own boss"); teaching real terms (anchoring, decoy,
endowment effect) with plain definitions per the teach-real-terms
principle; grounding price in usage data connects back to the analytics
posts, showing the system. Repackages the Pricing Psychology Cheat Sheet
toolkit item.

## Caption (exact)

Of all the steps between building something and earning from it, picking the price is the one that feels most like pulling a number from the air. Pricing psychology gives you three sturdy handles. 💶

Anchoring: a higher tier makes your real price feel reasonable. Decoy pricing: an option designed to lose makes the one next to it the obvious choice. The endowment effect: a trial and a named workspace make the product feel like theirs before the payment question arrives.

And the number itself comes from evidence you already have: your analytics show which features people use most, and that's what they'll pay for. Dalmie's data pointed at voting and deadlines, so her paid tier is built from exactly those, at $5 a month.
