## Instagram (Collab post with Kinga)

How to write a product update with AI people actually read 📣

You shipped the feature, and now you have to tell your customers, and everything you type starts with "We're excited to announce...". That generic launch post is what you get when you give an AI chat assistant just the feature name: it fills the gaps with guesses, and guesses read as filler.

The difference is context. Before you ask for the announcement, hand it the facts: what the feature does in one line, who it's for, the problem it solves, proof it works (a beta result, a customer quote), when it's available, and how to try it. Same request, real facts, and the announcement comes back concrete enough that people reply instead of scrolling past.

The checklist in the carousel is the reusable part. It works for feature updates, changelogs, and launch emails, any announcement where the writing is easy and the context is what's missing.

Most product updates written with AI open with "We're excited to announce..." and get skipped. What changes them is the context you hand the tool before you ask.

When you give a chat assistant just the feature name, it fills the gaps with guesses, and guesses read as filler. So you get the announcement every company has already posted.

Before you ask for the update, give it the facts:

1. What the feature does, in one line
2. Who it's for
3. The problem it solves
4. Proof it works (a beta result, a customer quote)
5. When it's available
6. How to try it

Same request, real facts, and the announcement comes back concrete: it opens with the value, sounds like a person, and gives people something to reply to.
