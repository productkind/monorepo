# Post 6: Launch early, grow steadily

**Pillar:** the calm view.
**Research trace:** the audience is over-served on first-hour hype and
abandoned at "week 4"; a calm, steady growth rhythm is our positioning
(possibility over urgency, no manufactured deadlines); the Buffer story
models launching as learning; "one improvement a week" is a concrete,
promised-outcome cadence that fits the 9:45pm window. Draws on the course's
final challenge and the Production-Ready Launch Checklist toolkit item.

## Caption (exact)

The launches you see on social media look like fireworks: one big day, a countdown, a splash. Most real products grow another way. 🕯️

Buffer started as a landing page with nothing behind it; the sign-ups proved people wanted it, and then it got built. Your version can be as small as sharing your app with three groups of real users, keeping a list of what they say, and shipping one improvement a week.

One improvement a week adds up: over three months that's twelve improvements shaped by real feedback, which beats a season of polishing alone. And the pace fits around a job and a family, which fireworks rarely do.
