# Claire Vo / Solo Founders podcast — small businesses, personal goals

**Angle:** Reflection on the Solo Founders episode with Claire Vo (ChatPRD). Lower capital requirements make much smaller businesses viable, so measure a business by whether it fits your personal goals, not by scale.
**Key point:** Small business means small overhead; know where your time goes (Kinga's spring tracking showed 629 app switches on the busiest day, messaging cutting into every work block) so you can protect time for the work that fits your goals.

---

"There is an opportunity to commercialise much smaller businesses because the capital required is much lower."

That's Claire Vo on the Solo Founders podcast. She's the founder of ChatPRD and its only full-time human employee with no venture capital funding.

I don't think a business has to be big to be worth building. But that's what we're constantly told: scale, grow your headcount, grow your revenue. What's the end goal, though? A small business comes with small overhead. You don't have to break your back increasing revenue just to fund the people you hired.

So the question I'd rather ask is "does this fit my personal goals?" And that means knowing where your time goes.

This spring I tracked my working days, and the biggest pattern was the context switching – no wonder running a two-person company. On my busiest day I changed apps 629 times, and messages cut into every block of work. Seeing this made it easier to protect time for the work that fits my goals. Try it for a week.

The whole episode is really about that same question, finding what makes you fulfilled, it's worth a listen.

I'm curious, if you stopped measuring success by scale, what would you work on?
