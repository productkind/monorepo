I wish PRDs were dead, even though I dislike the "X is dead" trope on LinkedIn.


When I watch people advise others on how to vibecode something, it almost always starts with a PRD. That is some of the worst advice you can give.


PRDs were never a good idea. Before AI, a PRD was one more way to avoid the conversation a team actually needed to have. Shared understanding between the business and the product team comes from talking to each other and surfacing the blind spots we all carry. A single document has never delivered that. At best, it starts the conversation.


Whenever someone handed me a PRD, the next thing I did was ask for a call to have the real conversation. Most of the time, what we worked out together looked quite different from the original document.


With vibecoding, the problem gets worse. If you are vibecoding, you will probably ask an AI assistant to write the PRD for you. The AI will likely produce a document full of context that is irrelevant, or even contradictory, to the tool you are about to feed it into. Because they are vibecoders, they trust the AI's output since it seems smart. I do the same with topics where I am not the expert. I have seen people hand tech stack decisions to Lovable without realizing that the stack they described isn't possible in Lovable.


App builder tools usually do not ask questions or seek shared understanding. They try to build whatever you prompted and fill the gaps as best they can. You end up with an output full of features that have nothing to do with what you had in mind.

So what do I recommend instead?

Think about your first feature only. Go back and forth with an AI assistant to work through the edge cases and get to a clear picture of what you want. Then start instructing your app builder to create that feature, step by step. Don't dump the whole vision on it. Tell it the happy case first. Test it. Check whether it works the way you pictured. Then go edge case by edge case, testing continuously as you go.


I promise you will get further and feel far more confident in what you build than you would by starting with a PRD.
