# Research: What performs on TikTok and Instagram (carousels, captions, reels, non-follower reach)

Researched: 10 July 2026, via deep-research workflow (21 sources fetched, 103 claims extracted, 25 top claims adversarially verified: 22 confirmed, 3 refuted).

Goal: grow follower numbers on both platforms and reach non-followers, ultimately guiding them to Little Parrot.

## Summary

Non-follower reach is won per-post, not per-account, on both platforms: Instagram states its ranking no longer favours large follower counts, and TikTok's official docs confirm follower count is not a direct For You feed factor. A 2-person brand can reach new audiences on the strength of individual posts. The practical split: short, high-completion video is the discovery engine on both platforms (Reels lead reach across every follower tier on Instagram; on TikTok 96% of viewing happens in the For You feed and completion/watch time is the heaviest signal). Carousels are Instagram's engagement and saves workhorse, especially strong for education accounts, and belong primarily on Instagram rather than TikTok. Captions and sounds are ranking metadata, not decoration.

## How discovery actually works (confirmed, high confidence)

### Instagram

- Instagram runs a **separate ranking algorithm per surface** (Feed, Stories, Explore, Reels). Explore is the surface built for non-follower discovery: most Explore content comes from accounts the viewer doesn't follow, and broad popularity signals (likes, comments, shares, saves across the platform) weigh much more heavily there than in Feed or Stories.
- **Reels ranking** predicts reshare likelihood, watch-through, and likes. Mosseri's 2025 framing names watch time, sends per reach, and likes per reach as the top signals, with **sends (DM shares) most important for reaching new audiences**. Make things people want to forward to a friend.
- Instagram states content ranking **no longer primarily favours accounts with large follower counts**; creators of all sizes get the same opportunity to reach new audiences. (Platform self-description, but consistent with Mosseri's 2025-2026 descriptions of exploration-based ranking that tests content with small audiences and expands on engagement.)
- Since early 2025, **reels up to 3 minutes** are eligible for recommendation to non-followers in the Reels tab and Explore, so tutorial-length educational video qualifies for discovery.
- **Adding audio to a carousel makes it eligible to appear in the Reels tab**, an extra distribution surface to new audiences beyond the feed.
- **Trial Reels** show a reel to non-followers first (followers don't see it), a purpose-built discovery-testing tool. Caveat: requires a public account with 1,000+ followers, so not available to Little Parrot until it crosses that threshold.

### TikTok

- The For You feed scores each video per-viewer on predicted likelihood of liking, sharing, commenting, finishing, or skipping, and serves the highest scorers. **Neither follower count nor an account's past video performance are direct ranking factors**; distribution is per-video. (Large accounts still benefit indirectly via their follower base's engagement signals.)
- **Users spend 96% of their time in the For You feed** (TikTok's own figure), so algorithmic reach is effectively the whole game; the Following feed barely matters.
- **Watch time and completion are the heaviest signals**: watching a longer video from beginning to end is TikTok's own example of a strongly weighted indicator. TikTok's Creator Academy (April 2026) names three levers: watch time (trim lulls, quick cuts), trends balanced with authentic posts, and sparking comments/shares.
- TikTok applies a **similarity check** that swaps out top-ranked videos that are too alike (e.g. same sound), so pure trend-copying with the same sound can get replaced in a viewer's feed. Trend participation helps, but with a distinct angle.

## Carousels

- On **Instagram**, carousels are the engagement and retention workhorse: **9x more saves than single-image posts** (Metricool 2026, 24.3M posts) and they outperform single images on every major metric. Corroborated by Socialinsider Q1 2026 (highest engagement rate of any format, 0.55% vs 0.35% for single images) and Rival IQ 2025 (carousels led engagement in several industries including Influencers). The 9x multiplier is one vendor's raw average; the direction is multi-source.
- The carousel engagement edge is **not** a large-account-only phenomenon (the claim that it only kicks in above 500K followers was refuted 0-3 in verification).
- Carousels perform far better on Instagram than TikTok: according to Metricool, Instagram carousels get 4.7x more views and 5.8x more interactions than TikTok carousels, and video remains TikTok's strongest format by a wide margin (5-6x carousels). Medium confidence (single-vendor multipliers, 2-1 verification vote), but the direction is corroborated by Statista and Buffer. **For cross-posting: carousels belong primarily on Instagram; TikTok effort should go into video.**
- Add audio to Instagram carousels for Reels-tab eligibility (see above). Whether that eligibility delivers reach comparable to native Reels is an open question.

## Reels / short video

- **Instagram Reels lead in reach across ALL follower tiers**, including 0-1K accounts (Metricool/HypeAuditor, 700M posts, Jan-Jun 2025), making them the most effective path to new audiences. Buffer's independent large-sample analysis agrees (Reels reach ~2.1x more non-followers than any other format).
- Reels generate **4x+ the interactions of single-image posts**, but average Reel watch time is only **8.5 seconds** (doubled year-over-year), so the hook must land almost immediately.
- Reels ranking is optimised for entertainment; the top levers are **sends per reach, watch time, and likes per reach**. Content that a viewer would DM to a friend ("you have to see this") travels furthest.
- On TikTok, completion rate rules: shorter videos that get watched to the end beat longer ones that get skipped. Front-load the hook, trim every lull.

## Captions

- On **TikTok**, creator-controlled metadata (captions, sounds, hashtags) is one of the three official ranking signal categories, alongside user interactions and device/account settings. **Caption keywords and sound choice are ranking inputs, not decoration.** Practitioner analyses in 2025-2026 suggest keyword captions, spoken audio, and on-screen text now outweigh hashtags within this category, though TikTok publishes no relative weights.
- Write captions (and on-screen text, and spoken words) with the search phrase a target learner would type, e.g. "build an app without coding".
- Coverage gap: the verified evidence says little about caption *craft* (length, hook phrasing, hashtag count) beyond captions being ranking metadata. What surfaced on this was low-quality blog material that didn't survive prioritisation. Treat detailed caption-formula advice found online as unverified.

## Education content specifically

- **TikTok was the highest-engagement platform** of the four in Rival IQ's 2025 benchmark (despite median engagement falling ~34% year-over-year).
- **Education accounts are standout performers on both platforms**: Higher Ed had the highest TikTok engagement of any industry (4x+ the all-industry median, with modest posting activity) and was the clear Instagram engagement winner despite below-median posting frequency. Educational content connects without high posting volume. Transferability caveat: "Higher Ed" means universities with captive student/alumni audiences; treat as directional encouragement, not a benchmark.

## Practical playbook for Little Parrot

1. **Lead with short video for discovery on both platforms.** Hook in the first 1-2 seconds (Instagram average Reel watch time is 8.5s); optimise for completion on TikTok and for sends on Instagram ("worth forwarding to a friend who's stuck" is the bar).
2. **Use save-worthy educational carousels on Instagram** (step-by-step, checklists, "keep this" material) as the engagement/saves engine; add audio so they're Reels-tab eligible. Don't invest carousel effort on TikTok; post video there.
3. **Treat captions, on-screen text, and spoken words as searchable metadata**, phrased in the words a non-technical learner would search.
4. **Post quality over volume.** Education accounts win with modest cadence; every post is its own shot at non-follower reach regardless of follower count.
5. **At 1,000 Instagram followers, start using Trial Reels** to test discovery content on non-followers before it hits the main grid.
6. Tutorial-length content works: reels up to 3 minutes are discovery-eligible on Instagram, and TikTok rewards longer videos *if* they hold watch time.

## Refuted in verification (do not cite)

- "Watch time is the single most heavily weighted TikTok ranking factor, ahead of likes/shares/comments" (0-3). It is among the heaviest signals but the single-top-factor framing didn't hold.
- "Carousels only outperform above 500K followers" (0-3).
- "Only 21% of Instagram accounts under 10,000 followers are growing in 2026" (0-3).

## Open questions

- What specific caption characteristics (length, keyword/SEO usage, hook phrasing, hashtag count) measurably improve discovery reach in 2025-2026? Verified evidence establishes captions are ranking metadata, not what makes a caption perform.
- Do Instagram carousels with audio in the Reels tab actually achieve non-follower reach comparable to native Reels?
- What posting frequency optimises growth for accounts under 10K followers? (The one small-account growth statistic surfaced was refuted, leaving no verified benchmark.)
- Do TikTok photo-mode carousels serve niche educational content for small accounts? Signals conflict: higher engagement *rates* within TikTok, but far lower absolute reach than video.

## Caveats

- TikTok's newsroom algorithm explainer dates from June 2020 and Instagram's ranking explainer from May 2023; both remain the platforms' canonical live explanations and are corroborated by 2025-2026 statements, but they predate the research window.
- Both platforms' "small accounts have equal opportunity" statements are self-descriptions, not independently audited.
- Metricool figures (9x saves, 4x interactions, 4.7x/5.8x cross-platform, 8.5s watch time) come from vendor press releases: large samples but raw averages not controlled for account size or niche, and Instagram vs TikTok "views" are counted differently. Treat multipliers as that study's measurements, not universal constants.
- Rival IQ engagement rates are follower-normalised brand-account metrics, not non-follower reach.
