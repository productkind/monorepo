LITTLEPARROT.APP03 / 07

Add a second part to the prompt: how it should sound, and how long it should be.

YOUR PROMPT 
Write a Slack message to the development team asking about the status of the checkout page slow loading issue.
Tone: friendly, warm, collaborative, confident.
Style: 1 to 2 sentences, conversational Slack style, direct ask for current status or ETA, end with a brief thanks.
↑

Part one says what you need. Part two specifies how it sounds.

LITTLEPARROT.APP05 / 07

The four levers, for any message where tone could go wrong:

TONE
State it explicitly: formal or casual, peer or mentor.
AUDIENCE
Name who it's for; depth and jargon follow.
SAMPLE
Show a sample of your writing to mirror.
LENGTH
Specify it: "2 sentences", "3 paragraphs", "≤200 words".

They work in every chat assistant: ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Gemini.

LITTLEPARROT.APP06 / 07

What tone and style can do, and what they can't:

✓
Make the output sound like you.
Keep your messages consistent.
✗
Reduce factual errors. A message can sound exactly like you and still be wrong.

Checking the facts is a separate skill (and a separate post).
