## LinkedIn (Kinga's profile, 3x/week)

- **[C] The gap that survives equal access.** The HBS finding: women are ~22%
  less likely to use generative AI even when access is equal, and identical
  AI-assisted work is rated 9% less competent when a woman did it. Open on the
  Kenya experiment, close on what this means for how we teach. Citable,
  mission-proving, made for resharing by community leaders.
- **[MM] The agent said it fixed it. It didn't.** The doom loop, named. Teach
  diagnosis before fixing: ask the AI to explain what's wrong and where before
  letting it touch anything. Use a real stuck moment of ours or a learner's.
- **[MM] The credits post.** Why credits die faster than expected (every retry
  costs, vague prompts cost more) and the three habits that stop the burn:
  plan first, small steps, bookmark before experiments. ROI framing lives here.
- **[M] The prompt recipe our learners repeat back.** Context, audience, tone,
  length. Show one real before/after from our own work. Saveable.
- **[W] Learner story series.** One builder per post, with consent, in the
  native arc: permission, surprise, beneficiary. Start with the life-shaped
  builds (dementia care app, eNPS tool, the SheBuilds live build). Credit her,
  link her.
- **[MM] "Guys, I'm under attack."** Retell the Enrichlead story kindly (frame
  the learner's pain, not the builder's failure), then the three checks before
  publishing an app. Security as what makes it "real", not advanced content.
- **[M] Building in public, continued.** The two-person-company thread from the
  June calendar: how the next system (this content strategy, the course
  pipeline, the fan-out) works, with real prompts.
- **[C] "Use AI", said the company. For what?** The mandate-without-method
  observation from the conference posts, extended with what a useful mandate
  looks like: one worked example beats any policy.
- **[W] The 1980s post.** Women were entering computer science faster than men
  until PCs were marketed to boys. One chart, one parallel to now, one thing
  each reader can do. (Check chart data before publishing.)
- Format note: single image or plain text; carousels only after ~5K followers.

### "How I use AI for [life thing]" episode areas

Chosen for recurrence in the audience's life, a visible before/after, and an
iPhone/Apple Intelligence tie-in where possible. Each episode ends with the
bridge: the same skill (specificity, context, small steps) is how you build an
app later.

1. School and family logistics: photo of the school letter, out come the dates,
   the calendar entries, the packing list. The mental-load capture.
2. Meal planning and the shopping list (the use she already has; show the
   upgrade from "give me recipes" to a specific, constrained prompt).
3. Deciphering official paperwork: insurance letters, contracts, bills, and
   drafting the appeal or complaint reply.
4. Doctor's appointment prep: turn worries into a question list, summarise a
   letter to understand it (prepare, never diagnose).
5. The subscription audit and comparing offers: money admin with a number at
   the end.
6. Travel planning under real constraints (toddler, budget, three free hours).
7. Wardrobe and colour analysis (the validated engagement winner in this
   niche).
8. Kids' occasions: birthday party logistics, gift shortlists.
9. iPhone-native wins: summarised voice notes, priority notifications, photo
   cleanup (ties to the life-admin course).
10. Career admin: the CV refresh and LinkedIn About rewrite (bridge for the
    career-changer segment).

## TikTok (new channel, experiment)

Not in `channels.md` yet; add a block when we commit. Rationale: the research
found no established female vibe-coding educator anywhere, and short video is
where that lane is most visibly empty (Rachel Woods proves practical,
non-technical AI TikTok scales; the "corporate girlie" AI content there is
described as hollow, so substance stands out). Cost control: record once,
publish as both TikTok and Instagram Reels; screen recordings over produced
video; review after 8-10 posts.

- **[M] Real-time mini-builds.** Screen-recorded "building a [life-shaped app]
  with no code" in 60-90 seconds, including the hiccup and the fix. The
  imperfection is the differentiator against polished demo-bro content.
- **[MM] Doom-loop rescues.** Take one genuinely stuck moment, show the
  diagnosis prompt, show it working. Serialisable: "stuck moment #4".
- **[C] One-take myth posts.** "You don't need to become a developer to build
  software", the trust-gap stat, "why all Lovable apps look the same (and the
  fix)".
- **[W] Duet/stitch learner builds** (with consent) adding the how behind the
  result.
