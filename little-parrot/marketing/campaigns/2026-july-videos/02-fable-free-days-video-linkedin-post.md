# Fable 5 Free Days: Models Are Not a Silver Bullet (Video Post)

LinkedIn post from Kinga's personal account, accompanying a 3-second video clip about Claude's new model, Fable 5, being free to try for three days. The clip is the visual hook; the post carries the message: a new model won't solve your business strategy, what matters is the context you give it and the skills you define that describe what a good output looks like for you.

Video file: `Fable_is_free_for_3_more_days.mp4`

## Final post

All my social media feeds this week are the same countdown: Anthropic's new model, Fable 5, is free until July 7, so drop everything and squeeze as much out of it as you can.

I have been using Fable all week, and what got me useful output was the same thing as always: the context I gave it and how clearly I could say what good looks like for me.

For this post, that meant spelling out my writing style, my message, and the exact joke I wanted at the end. A clear brief on last month's model gets you further than an ambiguous prompt on this one.

So no, I'm not rearranging my week around a countdown.

And if you're wondering how the shiny new model performs: it took me an embarrassing number of rounds to get Fable to help write this short post. Three more days of that. Enjoy 😅

## Earlier version (personal account, longer, not used)

Anthropic's new model, Fable 5, is free to try for three more days, and my feed is full of posts implying it will sort out your business by Monday.

For most everyday tasks we use AI for, the quality of the output is way less effected by the model. Is it Opus 4.8 or Fable 5? I didn't see a huge difference in its research output.

The most important parts are:
1. The context you give it
2. Your definition of what goods look like


I've been using it since it came out. It is a noticeably better model. And the two things that decide the quality of what I get from it are the same two things as with every model before it:

The context I give it. A model knows nothing about your customers, your constraints, or your goals until you tell it.

A written description of what good looks like. At productkind we keep ours in small documents called skills: our tone of voice, our course checklists, what a good LinkedIn post looks like to us. When a new model arrives, we hand it the same documents, and the stronger model applies our judgement better. The thinking in those documents stays ours, whichever model runs them.

No model will decide your business strategy for you, this one included.

If you're trying Fable this weekend, here's a place to start: write one paragraph describing what a good result looks like for the task you do most often, and give it that alongside your request. You'll reuse that paragraph long after the free days end.
