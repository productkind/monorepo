# Instagram: Build a Valuable Product (6 carousels)

Six Instagram carousels built from the **Build a Valuable Product: How to
Break Down Your Idea for Vibe Coding** course
(`vibe-coding-product-management-00`) and its toolkits, shaped by
`marketing/content-strategy/audience-research.md` and the content strategy.

## Why these six (research trace)

- **"An end-to-end idea-to-app framework" sits in our strongest demand
  data**, and this course is that framework. The six posts walk its
  narrowing funnel: problem → person → journey → map → MVP → test.
- **The audience are idea people** ("I can blueprint a whole app out of an
  idea but I can't code"). These posts honour that expertise: the problem
  you understand is the valuable part, and these are the tools that turn it
  into build decisions.
- **This is Kinga's PM lane**, shared rather than taught from above: real
  product-management frameworks (problem statements, personas, story
  mapping, MVP scoping, usability testing) with real worked examples, the
  method-shown register that the research says is first-in-category.
- **All six repackage existing toolkit items**, per the reuse-first rule,
  and each is a saveable template, cheat sheet, or checklist.
- The worked example throughout is life-shaped (Sarah's book club), the
  inclusion signal the research flags, and the opposite of techbro demo
  apps.

## Posting notes

- Account: Little Parrot Instagram. Cadence 1 to 2 per week, so this set
  covers three to four weeks. Suggested order: post in numbered order; it
  mirrors the course's narrowing funnel and each post references the
  previous one's output.
- CTAs name the full course title (Build a Valuable Product: How to Break
  Down Your Idea for Vibe Coding) and LittleParrot.app, because the
  Instagram bio holds only 5 links and we have more courses than slots.
  Wherever the course link is actually placed (a bio slot, stories,
  comment replies), use:
  `https://littleparrot.app/e16d932e-ac1f-4514-b5da-42629acf39ae/course-overview?utm_source=instagram&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=valuable-product`
  (add `&utm_content=post-1` etc. per post for per-post tracking).
- The course's first challenge (Craft a Problem Statement) is free; CTAs
  say so.
- Carousel slides: 1080 x 1350 (4:5 portrait), same visual system as the
  other July sets.
- Overlap notes: the course's AI-builder prompt template is deliberately
  skipped here (the build-first-app campaign's post 1 covers that ground).
  Post 6 (user test) is pre-launch usability testing with a task, distinct
  from the build-first-app campaign's first-user script and the
  launch-and-grow campaign's post-launch question bank; space them out and
  they read as a series on listening to users.
