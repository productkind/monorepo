# My notes

At both conferences, on the stage, people displayed how AI made them more productive, sped up their operations. But of course, no practical demos were shown that could enable participants to take away some of these techniques and implement it in their own workflows or organisations.

Meanwhile, when I had discussions in the audience, most people were tired of all this AI hype. Many people mentioned that their company tells them to "use AI" but fails to give any guidance. So it becomes another task to use AI for who knows what. Just to use AI.

### John Moriarty
Phrased productivity as creating 3-times as mant pull requests. This is an output mindset. Do those PRs create value or are we just creating more stuff that overwhelme users?

Also, creating 9 products in 2 months is great, but that's not the metric we should strive for. It's an output, again. Do they create revenue for the company? Is anyone using them? Are their lives easier because of this new product?

### Brian Cox

Why are we at university? Find your own way of understanding. Turn it into new knowledge. The goal is to deepen my understanding, not score well on exams.

This is how I understand it, but then you should go, use ai and different textbooks and internalise it to truly understand it. Find your own way to understand it.
