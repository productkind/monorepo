# Post 2: Fix, revert, or start fresh?

**Pillar:** the messy middle.
**Research trace:** the doom loop ("the agent said it fixed it, it isn't
fixed") is the audience's No. 1 pain and the biggest competitive gap; the
missing skill is judgement about what to do when something breaks;
"bookmark before experiments" is one of the course's own credit-saving habits;
normalising breakage ("things will break, and that's okay") is
confidence-by-design.

## Caption (exact)

Every builder we know has watched a change break something that worked five minutes ago. It will happen to you too, and it says nothing about your ability. What helps is knowing your three ways out. 🧭

Fix it when you can describe the problem precisely. Revert when you can't untangle it (Lovable saves every version, so you can always step back). And start fresh when the approach itself came out wrong.

That last one feels wasteful at first, and it's often the fastest route. Your prompt is the valuable thing, not the output. Rewrite the prompt, get a cleaner build.

One habit makes all of this painless: bookmark your version before you experiment, the way you'd save a game before a boss fight. 🎮
