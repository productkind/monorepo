## Caption (exact)

A useful mental model for the chat assistant you use every day: an extremely well-read autocomplete. It breaks your words into tokens (small pieces of text) and predicts what comes next, one token at a time, drawing on patterns from everything it has read. 🔮

Three things follow from that, and they change how you use it. Hallucinations are built in: a plausible-sounding answer can be confidently wrong, so verify what you'll act on. Asking the same question twice can give different answers, because it samples among likely continuations, so re-asking is variation rather than confirmation. And prompting techniques work precisely because of the mechanism: tone, audience, length, and context all steer which patterns it draws from.

None of this makes the tool less useful. Knowing the mechanism means you use it with intention instead of superstition.
