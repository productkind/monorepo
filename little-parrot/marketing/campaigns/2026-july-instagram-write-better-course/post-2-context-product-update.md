## Caption (exact)

Between "shipped it 🎉" and a wall of Jira tickets, there's a release update to write, and it decides whether anyone outside your team understands what you built. 📣

The difference between the update people skim and the one they react to is usually the context you hand the chat assistant, rather than the writing itself. Feature name alone gets you "We're excited to announce...", which reads as filler because it is: the model guessed everything it didn't know. Give it the customer request behind the feature, the beta feedback, the release date, and the team to credit, and the update comes back concrete.

The checklist on slide 5 is the reusable part; it fits any feature, any company.
