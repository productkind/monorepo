# Instagram posting sequence: all course carousels

The master schedule for the 49 carousels across the eight course campaigns.
Two posts a week (Tuesday and Friday), 25 posting weeks, with the two
Christmas weeks skipped. Suggested anchor: week 1 starts Tuesday
**21 July 2026** (giving two weeks from creation for slide design); shift
the anchor freely, the sequence holds.

## How the sequence is built

1. **Two lanes per week, different entry points.** Weeks mix a
   builder-journey post with a skills or work-wedge post, so any given week
   serves more than one audience and no month reads as a single topic.
2. **Campaign-internal order is never broken.** Each campaign's posts
   appear in its README's suggested order.
3. **The Dalmie storyline stays chronological across campaigns:** BFA
   (build it, weeks 1-7) → LG (launch it, weeks 8-15) → BB (earn from it,
   weeks 22-25). VP (weeks 16-21) is idea-stage content, but by week 16
   followers have watched a build, so its captions read naturally as
   "planning your next idea".
4. **Spacing constraints from the campaign READMEs are honoured:**
   - BFA-2 (fix/revert/start fresh, week 3) and DB-1 (bug loop escape,
     week 8) sit 4+ weeks apart.
   - SB-3 (where is the problem, week 6) precedes the debugging campaign
     by two weeks.
   - The listening-to-users trio is evenly spread: BFA-6 (week 7),
     LG-5 (week 14), VP-6 (week 21).
   - SB-6 and DB-6 (both explain RLS) sit 4 weeks apart (weeks 19 and 15).
5. **WB (work wedge) runs every other week for the first 11 weeks**, so
   the account serves the desk-job audience from day one without diluting
   the builder story.
6. **BB lands in January**, which suits it: new-year energy and the
   "earn from what you built" promise are a natural pairing.

## Course CTAs and the bio

Decision (2026-07-06, see
`content-strategy/instagram-carousel-research.md`): every post's final
slide and caption name the **full course title** and say it's on
**LittleParrot.app**. The bio holds only 5 links, so posts never depend on
"link in bio", and the schedule needs no bio updates on posting days.

- Use the bio's 5 slots for evergreen links (suggestion: the two wedge
  courses' free challenges, the course catalogue, the newsletter, the
  latest campaign's course, rotated monthly).
- Each campaign README keeps its UTM URL; use it wherever the course link
  is actually placed (a bio slot, stories, comment replies to "where do I
  find it?").

## Production notes

- **Design in campaign batches, two weeks ahead of the schedule.** Batching
  keeps each campaign's visual motif consistent (the sticky-note wall, the
  bakery, the chat mockups) and beats designing post by post.
- Week 1 needs BFA-1 and WB-1 designed by 21 July; the SC comic post
  (week 16) only needs the existing panel SVGs exported to PNG.
- Slide specs, alt text, and captions are final in each post file; captions
  can be scheduled in advance in Meta Business Suite or Buffer.

## Flexibility rules

- A missed slot is a missed slot, not a crisis (per the content strategy):
  shift everything down rather than doubling up.
- Swapping Tuesday and Friday within a week is always fine.
- If a post underperforms or a topic feels mistimed, swap it with the next
  post *from the same campaign*, never across campaigns, so internal order
  and the spacing constraints survive.
- Review at week 8 and week 16 against the strategy's question: which
  pillar drove saves, shares, and free-course starts? If one campaign
  consistently outperforms, pull its remaining posts earlier by whole-week
  swaps.
