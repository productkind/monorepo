# Post 03 — Mon 8 Jun

**Angle:** Don't ask AI for the whole course at once; work in stages with a checkpoint between each.
**Key point:** The staged process, with a real checklist example, is the usable technique. The thinking and notes are ours.

---

When I first tried using AI to help build a course, I asked it for the whole thing in one go. What I got back read well, but it wasn't good enough to publish, and it was hard to see where it had gone wrong.

So I broke the work into stages. First I give it my own notes and ask it to turn them into an outline, and only the outline. I read that, fix what's off, and approve it. Then it turns the approved outline into the actual lessons. Each stage has to pass a short checklist before we move on, things like whether every step has a clear outcome for the learner.

Working in stages caught mistakes while they were still small. It's much easier to fix a problem in a five-line outline than to find it buried in three thousand words of finished lessons.

We've built our courses this way for a while now, and the quality holds because the process does, not because I got a single prompt exactly right. If your AI output is close but not quite there, it's often worth giving it less at once: ask for one stage, check it, then move on to the next.
