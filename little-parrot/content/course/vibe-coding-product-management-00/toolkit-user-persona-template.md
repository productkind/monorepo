## User Persona Template

A reusable template for creating a user persona. Fill it in for each product idea to keep your target user front and centre when making decisions.

### The template

Copy this and replace the bracketed text with your own details.

```
Persona: [Name], the [descriptive title that captures their situation]

About:
- [One sentence about who they are and their context]
- [Their relevant habits or comfort level with technology]
- [Anything else that shapes how they'd use your product]

Frustrations:
- "[Quote from their perspective about their biggest pain point]"
- "[Quote about a second frustration]"
- "[Quote about a third frustration]"

Goals:
- [What does success look like for them? Be specific.]
- [What do they want to be able to do?]
- [What experience do they want to have?]
```

### Filled-in example: Book Club Organiser

```
Persona: Sarah, the overwhelmed book club organiser

About:
- Started a book club with friends
- Loves reading but has limited free time
- Comfortable with basic apps, not technical but willing to try simple tools

Frustrations:
- "I can't find the book suggestion in the chat history"
- "We spend 30 minutes each meeting just figuring out what to read next"
- "Half the group doesn't vote because they miss the message"

Goals:
- Spend maximum 10 minutes per month on book club admin
- Get everyone to actually participate in choosing books
- Never lose a good book suggestion again
```

### Prompts to help you fill each section

If you're stuck on a section, try answering these questions:

**Name and title:**
What one phrase would describe this person's situation? Think "[Name], the [adjective] [role]" (e.g., "Sarah, the overwhelmed book club organiser" or "Marco, the time-pressed freelance designer").

**About:**
- What's their day-to-day context related to your product?
- How comfortable are they with technology?
- What constraints do they have (time, budget, skills)?

**Frustrations (write these as quotes):**
- What makes them sigh or roll their eyes?
- What takes them way too long right now?
- What workaround are they currently using, and why does it fall short?

Writing frustrations as quotes ("I can't find...") instead of descriptions ("The user can't find...") helps you see the world through their eyes.

**Goals:**
- If your product worked perfectly, what would change in their life?
- What would they be able to stop doing?
- What would they brag about to a friend?

### How to use your persona

Once you've created your persona, use it as a decision-making tool throughout your project:

- **When choosing features:** "Would Sarah actually use this, or is it just nice to have?"
- **When writing prompts for Lovable:** "What would Sarah expect to see on this screen?"
- **When something feels off:** "If I showed this to Sarah, would she understand what to do?"
