## Storyboard Template

A three-step framework for visualising how your user goes from struggling with a problem to having it solved by your product. Grab a piece of paper or open a drawing app, and sketch one panel at a time.

### The framework

Your storyboard has three parts, in this order:

| Panel | What to draw | Guiding question |
|---|---|---|
| **1. The Struggle** | Your user in the moment they experience the problem. Show the frustration, the chaos, the wasted effort. | What is your user doing when the problem hits, and how does it make them feel? |
| **2. The Happy Ending** | Your user after the problem is solved. Show them relaxed, productive, or celebrating. | What can your user do (or stop doing) once the problem is gone? |
| **3-5. The Moments In Between** | The key steps that take your user from struggle to happy ending. This is where your product appears. | What needs to happen, step by step, for your user to get from the struggle to the happy ending? |

### Why start with the struggle and the happy ending?

Starting with the beginning and the end makes the middle easier. Once you know where your user starts and where they need to get to, the steps in between become clearer. You're not inventing features from thin air; you're connecting two points you already understand.

### Filled-in example: Book Club Organiser

**Panel 1: The Struggle**
Sarah is at the kitchen table, scrolling through endless WhatsApp messages, trying to find a book title someone suggested last week. She looks overwhelmed.

**Panel 2: The Happy Ending**
Sarah is relaxing on the sofa with a book and a glass of wine. The book club coordination happened effortlessly and she has time to enjoy reading.

**Panels 3-5: The Moments In Between**
3. Sarah discovers the Book Club Organiser app and creates her book club in it.
4. Sarah and her friends add book suggestions and browse what everyone has recommended.
5. The group votes on their favourites and the app announces the winning book.

### Tips for sketching

- **Stick figures are perfect.** This isn't about art. It's about capturing the moment and the emotion.
- **One moment per panel.** Each panel should show one clear scene. If you're trying to fit two things in, split it into two panels.
- **Show emotions.** A frowning face in the struggle panel and a smiling face in the happy ending tell the story at a glance.
- **Write a caption under each panel.** A sentence or two explaining what's happening. The caption is just as useful as the drawing.
- **Keep it to 5-7 panels.** Enough to tell the story, not so many that it becomes overwhelming.

### After your storyboard

Your storyboard becomes the foundation for your user story map. The "moments in between" panels map directly to the activities and steps you'll break down in the next challenge.
