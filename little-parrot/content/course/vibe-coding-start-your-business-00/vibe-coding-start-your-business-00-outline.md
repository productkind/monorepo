# Course Outline: Start Your Business

**Full title:** Start Your Business: Turn Your Vibe Coded Product into Income

## Overview

This micro-course helps learners who have already built a digital product with an AI app builder take the next step: turning it into a real business. It covers validation, audience building, online presence, pricing, getting traffic, and the legal and financial basics.

The learner will go from "I built something cool" to "I have paying users and a plan to grow." No company registration required. No investors needed. By the end, the learner will have validated their idea, set up a professional online presence, chosen a pricing model, and started getting real traffic to their product.

The narrative follows Maya, a yoga instructor who built a class booking app with Lovable and wants to turn it into her first digital business.

---

## Structure

### 1. Validate Before You Invest
Find out if people want what you built, before spending a single pound on it.

- The "you don't need" list: a company, investors, a perfect product
- The one thing you do need: customers getting value from what you built
- How to test your message: tell people what your product does, one-on-one, and notice what clicks
- Consider pivoting early if there's no traction
- Reference to "Build a Valuable Product: How to Break Down Your Idea for Vibe Coding" for deeper product thinking
- Example: Maya describes her booking app to five yoga students and discovers they care most about the reminders feature, not the booking itself
- Exercise: Describe your product to three people and write down which part they respond to

### 2. Build Your Audience Before You Launch
Start collecting the people who'll become your first customers.

- Wait lists: why they work and how to set one up (collect emails before your product is "ready")
- Lead magnets: what they are and how to use a simple one to attract sign-ups
- Add a feedback or "report a bug" feature into your product to learn from early users
- User testimonials: start collecting them as soon as someone gets value (don't fake them)
- Small, consistent effort each week adds up
- Example: Maya adds a wait list to her app's landing page and offers a free "5-minute desk yoga" PDF to attract sign-ups. Within two weeks, she has 40 email addresses.
- Exercise: Choose one audience-building tactic (wait list, lead magnet, or feedback feature) and add it to your product

### 3. Set Up Your Online Home
Get a professional domain and email without spending more than $10-20.

- How to purchase a domain and what to consider when choosing one
- Google Workspace: set up a professional email with your domain
- Domain setup for sending emails (SPF, DKIM explained simply)
- Register on Google Business Profile to improve your visibility in search
- Budget rule: don't spend more than $10-20 at this stage
- Example: Maya buys mayaflow.com, sets up hello@mayaflow.com, and registers her Google Business Profile, all for under $15
- Exercise: Purchase your domain and set up a professional email address

### 4. Get Your First Visitors
Drive real people to your product without paying for ads.

- Where to list your product: Product Hunt, BetaList, and startup directories
- How each platform works and what to expect
- Mention the "Launch and Grow Your Lovable App" course for deeper publishing and growth strategies
- Why talking to people one-on-one is still your best marketing at this stage
- Example: Maya lists her app on three directories and writes a short post in a local yoga community Facebook group. She gets her first 20 visitors in a week.
- Exercise: Submit your product to at least one directory and share your link in one community where your users hang out

### 5. Decide What to Charge
Choose a pricing model and set up the basics of getting paid.

- Pricing strategies for a first product: free vs freemium vs paid
- Pricing psychology basics: how to pick a number that feels right
- What Stripe is and what you need to set it up (e.g., tax number). A separate course covers Stripe integration in detail.
- Simple, free banking solutions for receiving payments: Wise, Revolut
- Example: Maya chooses a freemium model: free for up to 10 bookings per month, $9/month for unlimited. She signs up for Stripe and connects a Wise account.
- Exercise: Write down your pricing model and the reasoning behind it. If you're ready, create a Stripe account.

### 6. Handle the Business Side
Sort out the legal and financial basics so you can operate with confidence.

- Find the easiest and cheapest legal framework in your location. Only register a company when you have solid revenue or a deal requires it. Setting up and running a company is costly.
- Talk to an accountant about your tax situation and the best structure for where you live
- When to invest more in your business vs keep it as a side project
- When (and whether) to quit your job: recommendations based on traction, not hope
- Example: Maya decides not to register a company yet. She books a one-hour call with an accountant to understand her tax obligations as a sole trader. She keeps her teaching job and dedicates five hours a week to growing her app.
- Exercise: Research the simplest legal framework for your location. Book a call with an accountant if you're earning (or about to earn) revenue.

## Narrative Arc

The course follows a practical progression from validation to operation:

1. **Test:** Does anyone want this? (Validate the idea with real people)
2. **Gather:** Start building your audience before you're "ready"
3. **Establish:** Set up a credible online presence on a tiny budget
4. **Attract:** Get real visitors to your product
5. **Earn:** Choose how to charge and set up payments
6. **Operate:** Handle the legal and financial basics to run sustainably

Each challenge builds on the previous one. Maya's yoga booking app serves as the running example throughout, showing each concept applied to a real scenario.

---

## Teaching Approach

- **Validation-first mindset:** Don't spend money or time on setup before confirming people want what you built
- **Budget-conscious throughout:** Every recommendation considers cost. The learner shouldn't spend more than $10-20 until they have traction.
- **Action over theory:** Each challenge ends with a concrete exercise the learner completes with their own product
- **No premature complexity:** Legal structures, company registration, and investor conversations are explicitly deferred until they're needed
- **Cross-references to other courses:** "Build a Valuable Product" for idea validation, "Launch and Grow Your Lovable App" for publishing, and a future Stripe integration course
