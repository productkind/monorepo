## 2. What Was People's Process During the Hackathon?

### Preparation & Planning Phase

Participants arrived with their prompt ready (Dr. Tracie Q. Gilbert: "My prompt is ready, I've got the playlist queued"). Many were in a planning/spec-writing mode before the gun went off.

Several started writing PRDs (Product Requirements Documents) and consulting external AI tools like Claude/Codex to generate knowledge base files or verify scope: "my codex AI is telling me that building a full application with full SaaS, multilingual marketing site, auth, workspaces, RBAC, history, audit trail, billing, SEO… is more than a 48hr build."

Participants were consciously narrowing scope: "I had to narrow mine down to an MVP with one happy path."

### Starting the Build

Participants began prompting Lovable immediately at the 2 PM UTC kick-off (or even before, as the platform was open). "So excited ladies!! I currently have a blank screen! I love what lovable is doing right now! building away."

Several shared that they were "front-loading" heavy work on Day 1: "Phew. Tbf, I am front-loading a lot of grunt work to today so it may slow down."

A number of participants had to start over:
- "It took me about 3 iterations for the design to land something passable, I started a different project totally fresh to see the difference." — Srklipsey
- "This is me and Lovable discussing vector vs. AI rich images — I just went ahead and started over." — Dr. Tracie Q. Gilbert
- "I am not doing too bad but I keep finding data gaps and haven't settled on a design I like." — Josephina Bonetto

Some used Claude in parallel to Lovable — the Claude MCP integration was specifically celebrated: "THANK YOU FOR THE CLAUDE MCP AND RELEASING IT RIGHT BEFORE SHEBUILDS SEASON THREE!!!!!"

Participants were using synthetic/dummy data to fill gaps: "I'm also doing lots of fake data, but I'm marking it all as 'test' so it'll be easy for me to delete later! So a lot of my users are 'Michelle TEST'."

Some uploaded PRDs to Claude to compress them into Knowledge Base snippets for Lovable: "I uploaded my prd to Claude, and prompted something like 'Based on my PRD, can you create text I can paste into the Lovable Knowledge section that's context for my project, limit is 10k characters.'" — seeyoonlater & michelle.

### Mid-Build Behaviour

Participants were actively testing, publishing, checking authentication, and asking peers for help. There was lots of peer-to-peer support (sharing tips about the Visual Editor, the Knowledge section, how to share previews before publishing, etc.).

A pivot mindset emerged: "Lesson learned! if you spend more than 30 min on a problem or going down a rabbit hole — pivot to a more simple solution. I spent about ~45min trying to figure out an email thing on my site and decided to just pivot to a simpler solution." — seeyoonlater

Some were building in public by posting to LinkedIn or sharing app previews: "Trying to build in public and just had to give Lovable such an amazing shoutout!"

Participants checked in on voice/video chat (SheBuilds Together) throughout the day to share screens, get unstuck, and cheer each other on.

One participant ran a security scan before publishing (Srklipsey). Some were working on Father's Day and managing competing family obligations: "yes having hard time devoting real time to this today with father's day."

Late-night builders persisted well past midnight: Marta Serafin posted at 11:38 PM from Barcelona still working; zislays at 11:45 PM: "guys i haven't dropped my laptop in like 4 hours."

## 3. What Were People's Needs That Weren't Met?

### Information Gaps & Unclear / Contradictory Rules

**The video requirement confusion was a significant unmet need.** Multiple participants had conflicting information (the welcome email said no video; the submission portal still showed it as required). There was a sustained stream of messages about this across the whole day, and one participant explicitly asked: "can someone please update this so other folks who may not be on discord aren't confused about the requirement for the video."

**The hackathon start time was confusing.** At least three participants had timezone issues and missed the kickoff or didn't know when to start building. One asked as late as 5:31 PM whether they could start, having "missed the virtual sess." Clarification of timezones (everything runs UTC) was reactive, not proactive.

**Judging criteria around functional depth vs. prototype was unclear.** Multiple separate participants asked versions of the same question throughout the day, indicating the FAQ or briefing materials didn't address this clearly enough: "For judging purposes, how much of the product is expected to be fully functional versus demonstrated through workflows and prototypes?"

**Kickoff recording was not immediately available.** At least two participants asked whether it would be recorded, indicating demand for async access that wasn't met in time.

---

### Platform / Tooling Limitations

**Project Knowledge cap (10,000 chars / no file upload).** Anastasia flagged that her PRD was ~50k characters and the Knowledge feature only supports copy/paste up to 10k. The community found a workaround (use Claude to compress), but this was not a Lovable-native solution.

**No direct integrations/connectors.** One participant building an AI content tool for healthcare noted Lovable doesn't have direct integrations and had to figure out how to use external APIs manually.

**Testing without publishing.** Participants expected to be able to test features like Google Auth without going live, but Lovable requires publishing first. This was confusing and felt like a missing feature.

**Visual editor limited to text changes.** Shannon could not change background colour via the visual editor as Lovable suggested — only text changes seemed to work. Required a workaround via the chat prompt instead.

**App crashing.** AshleyJ's desktop app kept crashing with no clear fix offered.

**Tester account creation.** One participant asked Lovable to create a test user and it generated a fake account they couldn't actually verify, requiring a workaround.

---

### Credits & Billing Anxiety

Multiple participants were anxious about running out of credits, especially for apps that use LLM API calls at runtime. The question of whether judges would be able to test apps if credits ran out was asked multiple times and caused stress: "So worried it won't be enough!" (Christine L). The system for how hackathon credits interact with plan credits was not clearly communicated upfront.

Participants using multiple workspaces didn't receive their 1,000 credits at launch and had to ask — the delay was not communicated proactively.

---

### Support Responsiveness

Migle explicitly flagged: "Just to double check, I left some questions for the team in portal, when can I expect answers? I understand it is weekend, so is it just tomorrow?" — indicating that questions submitted through the portal were not being answered quickly, and the Discord channel became a de facto support channel.

Kanyinsola posted: "Hi @Whitney didnt get any response so I would like to drop this here again" — showing that questions were getting lost in the fast-moving channel.

---

### Scoping & Complexity Guidance

Several participants struggled with scope — they arrived with ambitious plans and had no framework for what was achievable in 48 hours. The community (not the organisers) provided the key lesson: "I had to narrow mine down to an MVP with one happy path." There was no structured scoping resource or guidance proactively offered.
