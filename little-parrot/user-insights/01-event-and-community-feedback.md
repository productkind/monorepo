## Women's questions about AI — group AMA (Issue #176)

*Source: a group AMA about AI with 4 women working in the technology industry in business positions (CEO, COO, Head of Marketing, HR). These are the questions and pain points they raised — recorded as a group, no individual attribution.*

### Terms & questions raised

> - agentic AI
> - inside job descriptions: how can I understand from the developer's CV their experience
>   - rag systems
>   - evaluation
> - what to use for ChatGPT, Gemini and all? --> it's an LLM, but it's more like a platform, how should I mention these
> - in development you use agents, the platforms that it connects, what are these tools, how are agents managed
> - prompt engineer is not cool, is it not cool anymore
> - difference between prompt, context, AI engineer, AI-fist / AI-native egngineer
> - critical thinking from the developer's side - is it valid what ChatGPT says?
> - Reddit thread: all job interview people will get a math exercise to show if they can think analytical - what's your thought on it
> - panel example: what can't you give to AI (Claude Code) in software development?
> - our website can be done in Lovable - there should be a good CRM system with email database - that's what's holding me back to use Lovable to do our new website
> - If I ask a concrete fact - will it tell me? --> not clear what you can ask from LLMs that is reliable
> - How does the memory of ChatGPT work? --> they will refer to us as "digital product studio", not "AI development sg"
> - Tell me examples of supervised and unsupervised learning
> - Why does it forget things if your previous conversation is sent to the LLM? --> context rot
> - Which one should I use: thinking mode, others?
> - Didn't know about "project" in ChatGPT or Claude
> - Where does the RAG process happen? Not in the ChatGPT interface.

### Pain points

> - There is too much info, it would be helpful with some guidance
> - What's possible with AI agents (e.g. Claude Code) / vibe coding
> - Not clear what you can ask from LLMs that you can rely on
> - Not understanding: Memory vs randomness of LLMs
